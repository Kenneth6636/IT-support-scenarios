Scenario 1: Ticket Follow-up Call

You: Hi Emma, this is Alex from IT support. I'm following up on your ticket about the printer issue.

Emma: Oh, hi. Yes, the printer keeps showing "Paper Jam" even though I've checked it.

You: Got it. Could you try turning the printer off, unplugging it for 30 seconds, and then plugging it back in?

Emma: Just tried it... it works now.

You: Great. I'll go ahead and close the ticket, but if the issue comes back, just ping me.

Key Phrases for This Scenario:

  • I'm following up on your ticket
  • The printer keeps showing [error message]
  • Could you try [troubleshooting step]
  • I'll go ahead and close the ticket
  • Just ping me (contact me)

Scenario 2: Remote Desktop Support

You: Hi David, I saw your request about the software not launching. Can I remote into your PC to check?

David: Sure, go ahead.

You: Thanks. I'm in now. I can see there's a pending update. Let's install that and restart.

David: Okay... oh, it's working now.

You: Perfect. I'll note in the ticket that it was caused by a missing update.

Key Phrases for This Scenario:

  • Can I remote into your PC
  • I can see there's a pending update
  • Let's install that and restart
  • I'll note in the ticket
  • Caused by a missing update

Scenario 3: Escalation to Tier 2

You: Hi Chris, I've tried all the basic troubleshooting for your database access issue, but it's still failing. I'll escalate this to our Tier 2 team.

Chris: Okay, please keep me posted.

You: Will do. You'll get an update once Tier 2 investigates.

Key Phrases for This Scenario:

  • I've tried all the basic troubleshooting
  • I'll escalate this to our Tier 2 team
  • Please keep me posted
  • You'll get an update once [action]
  • Tier 2 investigates

Scenario 4: Incident Response Call

You: Hi team, this is Alex. We've just been alerted about a potential phishing email that went to several employees.

Manager: What's the plan?

You: I'll block the sender at the mail gateway and send an advisory email to all staff right away.

Manager: Good. Keep security in the loop.

You: Understood. I'll coordinate with the security team throughout.

Key Phrases for This Scenario:

  • We've been alerted about
  • Potential phishing email
  • Block the sender
  • Advisory email
  • Keep [department] in the loop

IT Support Key Phrases

Common IT Support Phrases and Meanings:

  • "ping me" - Contact me
  • "pending update" - An available but not installed update
  • "note in the ticket" - Document the solution in the tracking system
  • "escalate to Tier 2" - Send to higher level support team
  • "keep me posted" - Keep me updated on progress
  • "incident response" - Process for handling security issues
  • "block the sender" - Prevent emails from this address
  • "advisory email" - Warning message to staff about an issue
  • "remote into your PC" - Connect to your computer remotely
  • "close the ticket" - Mark the issue as resolved in tracking system
